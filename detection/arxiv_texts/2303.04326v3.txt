Abstract
A possibility of a pseudovector-type quark-antiquark cond ensed phase, which leads to a quark
spin polarized phase, in the quark matter is investigated ta king account of the vacuum eﬀects
leading to the chiral symmetry breaking by using the Nambu-J ona-Lasinio model. Also, possible
Nambu-Goldstone modes on the pseudovector-type quark-ant iquark condensate and the tensor-
type quark-antiquark condensate, which also leads to the qu ark spin polarized phase, are investi-
gated.

1I. INTRODUCTION
One of recent interests with respect to the strong interaction go verned by the quantum
chromodynamics (QCD) may be to clarify the phase structure on th e plane spanned by the
temperature and density [1]. Especially, at ﬁnite density and low temp erature, some authors
have pointed out [2–5] that there exists a possibility of a quark spin p olarization due to a
pseudovector-type quark-antiquark condensation by using the low energy eﬀective model of
QCD such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model [6]. Also, as was sh own in the previous
work, the spin polarization may also occur due to a tensor-type qua rk-antiquark condensate
in the NJL model [7] and the magnetic moment density is evaluated in th e hybrid star with
a quark matter in the inner core of compact stars [8].
By carrying out the Weinberg transformation, the pseudovector condensate due to the
pseudovector interaction in the NJL model can be recast into the in homogeneous conden-
sate [9]. Under the inhomogeneous condensate, the excited modes , especially the Nambu-
Goldstone (NG) modes [6, 10, 11] have been investigated by the Lan dau-Ginzburg-Wilson
eﬀective Lagrangian [12, 13].
In this paper, a possibility of the existence of the pseudovector-t ype quark-antiquark
condensed phase in the quark matter is investigated and its possibilit y is indicated taking
account of the vacuum eﬀect that leads to the chiral symmetry br eaking, which may be
neglected in some previous investigations [4, 5]. Further, the massle ss Nambu-Goldstone
modes on the pseudovector condensate due to the psedovector interaction in the NJL model,
which leads to the quark spin polarization, are investigated directly.

shown that the pseudovector condensed phase may appear in a ce rtain region with respect

This paper is organized as follows: In the next section, the gap equa tions for both the
