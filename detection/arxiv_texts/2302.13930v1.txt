I. INTRODUCTION
The possibility of tuning the magnitude of the in-
ductance in superconducting circuits is paramount to
achieve the high-degree of control and exibility required
for quantum technology [1]. Two platforms have been
mostly used to achieve high values of inductance: ar-
rays of Josephson junctions (JJs) [2{5] or high{kinetic
inductance disordered thin lms [6, 7]. JJ devices are
characterized by very low dissipation rates, and for this
reason they are routinely used in circuit quantum electro-
dynamics (cQED) [8, 9]. Usually, JJ-based circuits still
exhibit large nonlinearities. To dilute such nonlinearity
suited for applications such as quantum{limited paramet-
ric ampliers, arrays of JJs sacrice compactness and add
fabrication overhead [10, 11].
Kinetic inductance is a promising novel resource for
quantum technology [11{14]. For instance, supercon-
ducting high{kinetic inductance (high{ Lk) thin lms
have been used for several cryogenic applications, rang-
ing from detectors [13, 15, 16] to ampliers [14, 17], and
lters [18, 19]. In dirty superconductor thin lms, the in-
ductance depends on the Cooper pairs carrier density [6],
and it can be tuned by controlling the chemical composi-
tion of the lms during the deposition. From a cQED per-
spective, the advantages of high{ Lkare manifold. Thin

or high{impedance, and thus a higher range of achiev-
able capacitive-coupling between dierent circuital ele-
ments [20]. In turn, this allows exploring several regimes
of light-matter interaction, from the strong to more ex-
otic ultrastrong couplings [21, 22]. Given the compact
nature of high{ Lklm resonators, thin lms can reduce
the dimensions of both readout and control apparatus,
and facilitate the realization of many-modes quantum de-
vices [23]. Key to the success of this technology within a
quantum framework is attaining regimes of low internal
losses, where long coherence times can be maintained.
Many disordered superconductors have shown high{
kinetic inductance, e.g., NbN [23, 24], NbTiN [14, 17, 25],
and TiN [26], or materials composed by microscopic eec-
tive Josephson arrays such as the emergent granular Alu-
minum (grAl) [27]. Several groups manufactured weakly-
disordered thin lms that present considerable quality
factors [26, 28]. Consequently, thin lms with moderate
Lkproved useful for quantum computing tasks [29], in
particular for optical communication [30], quantum key
distribution [31, 32], and quantum teleportation [33].
Large-scale quantum applications will, however, also
require a wide range of Lktunability and high control and
reproducibility of the superconducting building blocks.

The advantages of bias sputtering [35], i.e., applica-

a reduction of superconducting critical temperature and
grain size, permitting the deposition of a polycrystalline
material. The polycrystallinity improves the supercon-
ductor homogeneity and it eases the device realization.
The lm behaves as an amorphous material with respect
to etching procedures, and simultaneously maintains the
advantageous properties of crystalline superconductors.
For example, the low electron{phonon interaction time
is barely changed, with important repercussions, such
as enhanced maximum count rate in superconducting
single{photon detectors [35]. To optimize the lms prop-
erties, we ne-tune the nitrogen to argon partial pressures
in the chamber across several fabrication runs, with pres-
sure and substrate bias chosen to give the lest roughness
in the lms. These steps also improve the reproducibility
and yield of the samples.
