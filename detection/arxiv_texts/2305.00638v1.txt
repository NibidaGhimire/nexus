1 Introduction
The study of nonsimple closed geodesics on hyperbolic surfa ces plays an essential role on 2-
dimensional hyperbolicgeometry, spectral theory and Teic hm¨ uller theory. Oneinteresting question

k-geodesic for brevity), when kgoes to inﬁnity, does the minimal length of k-geodesics goes to
inﬁnity, and what is the growth rate.
There has been lots of works on the problem till now. Hempel sh owed in [7] that a nonsim-

number gets large ([3, Corollary 1.2]). In [2], Baribaud com puted the minimal length of geodesics
with given self-intersection number on pairs of pants.
