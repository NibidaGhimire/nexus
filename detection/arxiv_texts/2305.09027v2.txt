Abstract. In this paper we prove the existence of global solutions to th e inho-
mogeneous incompressible Navier–Stokes equations, whene ver the initial veloc-
ity belongs to some subspace of BMO−1, and the initial density is suﬃciently
close to 1in the uniform metric. This is a natural extension to the vari able
density case of the celebrated result by H. Koch and D. Tataru [21] concerning
the classical Navier–Stokes equations.

Let us include a short historical comment on tent spaces. To t he best of our
knowledge, elliptic tent spaces were ﬁrst introduced in the article [9]. Many im-
portant properties of tent spaces have been discovered in ar ticles [2], [3], [4]. The
parabolic counterpart of these spaces was ﬁrst considered a nd successfully ap-
plied recently to the homogeneous Navier–Stokes equations (1) by P. Auscher and
D. Frey in [2] (see also [5]). There, deep connections betwee n well-posedness in
BMO−1and tent spaces have been pointed out and, compared to the ori ginal arti-
cle [21] by H. Koch and D. Tataru, using tent spaces turned out to allow to handle
more general parabolic systems with, possibly, rough coeﬃc ients.
Note that the diﬃculty in the present paper is that the system under consider-
ation is not fully parabolic. This suggests that another fun ctional setting should
be considered and leads us to consider a new scale of tent spac es.
The rest of the paper unfolds as follow. In the next section, w e introduce all
the functional setting pertaining to tent spaces and establ ish a few key properties.
The third section is devoted to the proof of the fact that the m aximal regularity
operator is bounded on our tent spaces and to the boundedness of the Leray
projector on these spaces. In the fourth section we prove our existence result.
In appendix, we prove some auxiliary results like a density r esult for Uαand
comparison with Besov and Sobolev–Slobodeckij type spaces .
We ﬁnally mention one open question concerning Theorem 1. Na mely, it would
be interesting to ﬁnd out whether this result holds in the cas e of domains with
suﬃciently smooth boundary. The authors plan to attack this question in the
nearest future. To this end, it seems natural for us ﬁrst to es tablish a ‘general’
description of the spaces Uα, for instance as it was done in [26] for the scale of
Triebel–Lizorkin spaces.
